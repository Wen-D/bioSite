I AM... A daughter, a sister, a niece, a grandaughter, a friend, a nurse, a mentor, a muse, a STRONG woman.

About

Nicole Santana is an undergraduate clinical nurse from the Université de Montréal. She has 5 years of experience in nursing. She also teaches Anatomy classes at the same institution.

Nicole is currently working at the Institut de Cardiologie de Montréal in the mental health department and is currently working towards completing her master's degree in Nursing at the UM.

Her tasks at the ICM include providing direct care to patients, monitoring, evaluating and recording the medical condition of 15 patients per day.

Originally from Ecuador, Nicole, is fluent in English, French and Spanish and would like to one day learn a fourth language by osmosis. While she still has not decided what language she would like to learn next, she thinks she might end up living and working in Brazil and learning Portuguese.

She is the first child of 3 siblings. Her brothers, Michael and Rafael are attending working towards an EMT program and are being mentored by Nicole in developing and achieving professional expertise.

Nicole is an avid reader, a photography enthusiast and loves to travel. She lives in Chambly, Quebec with her boyfriend, Samuel and their 3 cats.

Fun Facts

When she is not working, Nicole likes to spend time with family and friends, being in nature, running, hiking, kayaking, gardening, herbing, knitting, learning and teaching. She likes to cook Hispanic food and entertain. She is an animal lover and has 3 cats named Mocha, Toby and Olie.

Volunteering

As a volunteer faculty facilitator, she is actively involved in helping students handle difficult situations involving both patients and their families.
